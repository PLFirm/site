Medical Malpractice Cases in El Paso

Medical malpractice can occur in various forms, and it affects victims in different ways. Here are ten examples of medical malpractice cases we handle:

Baby Brain Damage Cases: When medical professionals fail to provide appropriate care during childbirth, it can result in severe brain injuries to newborns.

Emergency Room Misdiagnosis: Mistakes made in the emergency room can lead to delayed treatment and worsened medical conditions.

Anesthesia Errors: Errors during anesthesia administration can lead to serious complications and even fatalities.

Surgical Errors: Mistakes made during surgeries can cause significant harm to patients, leading to extended recovery times or permanent disabilities.

Medication Errors: Incorrectly prescribed or administered medications can have harmful consequences for patients.

Misdiagnosis or Delayed Diagnosis: Failing to diagnose or delaying the diagnosis of a medical condition can lead to progression and complications.

Birth Injuries: Injuries sustained during childbirth due to medical negligence can have long-lasting effects on both the child and the mother.

Dental Medical Malpractice: Mistakes during dental procedures, such as wisdom tooth extraction, can cause unnecessary pain and complications.

Hospital-Acquired Infections: Infections contracted during hospital stays due to poor hygiene or inadequate care can lead to further health issues.

Negligent Post-Operative Care: Inadequate post-operative care can result in complications and prolonged recovery periods.

Seeking Compensation for Medical Malpractice

Injuries caused by medical malpractice can result in significant financial losses, emotional distress, and a reduced quality of life. Our goal is to help you recover compensation for:

  • Past, present, and future medical expenses.
  • Lost wages and reduced earning capacity.
  • Long-term rehabilitation and disability costs.
  • Pain and suffering and emotional distress.
  • Funeral expenses in cases of wrongful death.

If you are unsure about your legal options or are concerned about the cost of pursuing a claim, rest assured that we work on a contingency-fee-basis. This means you pay nothing upfront, and we only collect payment if we secure a settlement or judgment on your behalf.
