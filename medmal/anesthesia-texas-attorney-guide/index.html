When Anesthesia Goes Wrong: A Texas Attorney's Guide to Medical Malpractice

Recognizing the legal complexities in anesthesia-related malpractice cases

Robert Painter
August 17, 2024

As a Texas litigation attorney focusing on handling medical malpractice cases for plaintiffs, I’ve seen firsthand the devastating impact of negligence in the operating room, particularly when it comes to the administration of general anesthesia. This article aims to shed light on common issues surrounding medical negligence involving general anesthesia and the legal implications for affected patients and their families.

Understanding General Anesthesia

Anesthesiologist physicians and certified registered nurse anesthetists (CRNAs) administer general anesthesia medications to induce a state of controlled unconsciousness, essential for many surgical procedures. General anesthesia renders a patient unconscious and unable to feel pain during surgery. While generally safe when administered properly, errors in anesthesia can lead to severe complications, including permanent injury or death. Common Types of Anesthesia-Related Medical Negligence

  1. Dosage Errors

One of the most frequent forms of negligence involves incorrect dosing of anesthetic drugs. This can include:

  • Overdosing, which may lead to prolonged unconsciousness, respiratory depression, or other systemic complications
  • Underdosing, potentially resulting in anesthesia awareness, where patients regain consciousness during surgery but remain paralyzed and unable to communicate

Legal issues often center around adherence by the anesthesiologist or CRNA to standard protocols for drug administration and proper monitoring of the patient’s vital signs.

  1. Failure to Monitor Vital Signs

Continuous monitoring of a patient’s vital signs during surgery is crucial. Negligence can occur when the anesthesia team fails to:

  • Adequately monitor blood pressure, heart rate, oxygen saturation, and carbon dioxide levels
  • Respond promptly to changes in vital signs indicating distress

In legal terms, this often involves proving that the standard of care was breached by not following established monitoring guidelines or failing to act on clear warning signs.

  1. Intubation Errors

Intubation is the process of inserting an endotracheal tube (breathing tube) down the patientâ€™s throat (windpipe), to control the airway and manage breathing with a machine. Proper intubation is critical for maintaining an open airway during general anesthesia. Errors can include:

  • Esophageal intubation (inserting the tube into the esophagus instead of the trachea)
  • Delayed intubation leading to oxygen deprivation
  • Trauma to the throat or vocal cords during intubation

These errors can result in severe complications such as brain damage due to oxygen deprivation or long-term voice problems. Legally, the focus is often on the technique used and whether appropriate precautions were taken. Discover how a lawsuit alleged CRNA caused botched esophageal intubation, patient death.

  1. Failure to Assess Patient History

Neglecting to thoroughly review a patient’s medical history can lead to serious complications. This includes:

  • Overlooking allergies to anesthetic agents
  • Failing to account for interactions with current medications
  • Not considering pre-existing conditions that may affect anesthesia risks

From a legal standpoint, this often involves examining the pre-operative assessment process and determining whether all reasonable steps were taken to gather and consider relevant patient information.

The American Society of Anesthesiologists (ASA) has established clear guidelines for pre-anesthesia patient assessment, which set the standard of care in the field. According to the ASA, a thorough pre-anesthesia evaluation must include, at minimum: a review of medical records, a patient interview, a directed physical examination, and review or ordering of pertinent diagnostic tests. This assessment should cover the patient’s medical history, anesthesia history, medication history, allergies, and any relevant family history. The ASA also emphasizes the importance of documenting the results of this evaluation. Failure to adhere to these guidelines can be considered a breach of the standard of care. In legal proceedings, these ASA standards often serve as a benchmark for determining whether an anesthesiologist’s pre-operative assessment met the required level of care, making them crucial in establishing negligence in cases where inadequate patient history assessment led to complications.   1. Equipment Malfunction or Misuse

Anesthesia requires the use of complex equipment. Negligence can occur through:

  • Failure to properly maintain or calibrate equipment
  • Misuse of equipment due to lack of training or attention
  • Not having backup systems in place for critical equipment

Legal cases involving equipment issues often require expert testimony to establish whether the standard of care was met in equipment maintenance and operation.

Legal Implications of Anesthesia Negligence

Establishing Negligence

To prove medical negligence in an aesthesia case, a plaintiff (patient) has to have evidence to support four key elements:

  1. Duty of Care: Establishing that the anesthesiologist or CRNA had a professional duty to the patient.

  2. Breach of Standard of Care: Proving that the care provided fell below the accepted medical standard.

  3. Causation: Demonstrating that the breach directly caused the patient’s injury.

  4. Damages: Showing that the patient suffered harm as a result.

In Texas, as in many states, expert testimony is usually required to establish the standard of care and how it was breached in anesthesia negligence cases.

Comparative Negligence

Texas follows a modified comparative negligence rule. If a patient is found to be more than 50% responsible for their injury (e.g., by providing false or incomplete information about their medical history), they may be barred from recovering damages. This means that thorough investigation and documentation is crucial in building a strong case.

Statute of Limitations

In Texas, the statute of limitations for medical malpractice claims is generally two years from the date of the injury. However, there are exceptions, such as cases involving minors. It’s critical for patients to seek legal counsel promptly to ensure their rights are protected.

Challenges in Anesthesia Negligence Cases

Complexity of Medical Evidence

Anesthesia negligence cases often involve highly technical medical evidence. As a Texas medical malpractice attorney, I work closely with medical experts to interpret complex data from surgical records, anesthesia logs, and patient monitoring systems. This requires a deep understanding of both medical and legal principles to effectively translate technical information for judges and juries.

Multiple Parties and Shared Responsibility

In many cases, multiple healthcare providers may share responsibility for anesthesia-related injuries. This can include:

  • Anesthesiologists
  • Certified registered nurse anesthetists (CRNAs)
  • Surgeons
  • Hospital staff, including registered nurses or anesthesia or operating room techs.
  • Determining the extent of each party’s liability requires extensive investigation and careful thought.

Proving Causation

It’s not enough that an anesthesiologist or CRNA made a mistake during the general anesthesia process. A plaintiff must prove that such a mistake directly caused the patient’s injury.

Anesthesia defendants often try pointing blame at patientsâ€™ pre-existing conditions. Strong expert testimony and thorough medical analysis are crucial in establishing this critical link.

Prevention and Patient Advocacy

While legal action is sometimes necessary, preventing anesthesia-related injuries is always preferable. Patients can take steps to protect themselves:

  1. Provide a complete and accurate medical history
  2. Ask questions about the anesthesia process and risks
  3. Ensure their anesthesia is administered by qualified professionals
  4. Follow all pre-operative instructions carefully

As a medical malpractice lawyer, I consider part of my role as not just to litigating cases but also educating the public about patient rights and safety measures.

What steps to take next?

Medical negligence involving general anesthesia can have life-altering consequences for patients and their families. As a Texas attorney specializing in this area, I’ve seen the profound impact these cases can have. While the legal process can be complex and challenging, it plays a crucial role in holding healthcare providers accountable and improving patient safety standards.

If you or a loved one has suffered due to suspected anesthesia negligence, it’s important to seek legal counsel promptly. An experienced medical malpractice attorney can help navigate the complexities of these cases, ensure your rights are protected, and work towards securing the compensation you deserve.
